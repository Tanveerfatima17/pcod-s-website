PCOS:

PCOS stands for "Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome". It is a heterogenous disease characterised by Hyperandrogenism and chronic anovulation. Hyperandrogenism is defined by the state caused by excessive production and secretion of androgens. Anovulation happens when an egg (ovum) doesn't release from your ovary during your menstrual cycle.

Features based on which an adolescent girl can be diagonosed of 'being at risk' are "menstrual irregularity" and "clinical signs of excess male hormone in body". The symptoms will be discussed clearly later. Pelvis ultrasound are not needed for the diagnosis of PCOS in adolescent girls. The ones who are at risk and those with a confirmed PCOS diagnosis, both the condition should be managed with a healthy lifestyle interventions, awareness and therapeutic interventions targeting their symptoms. An increasing number of women in the reproductive age are suffering from this hormonal disorder. PCOS is usually considered a more serious condition.

PCOD:

PCOD can often be managed just by making informed lifestyle changes PCOD stands for "Polycystic Ovarian Disease". It is a medical condition in which the woman's ovaries produce immature or partially mature eggs in large numbers and over the time these become cysts in ovaries. Due to which the ovaries become bulky and large. Also releases abnormal amount of male hormones which causes infertility, hairloss, weight gain, irregular menstrual cycle. PCOD is curable through healthy diet and life style.

SYMPTOMS

  • Menestrual irregularity.
  • Fluctuations in weight.
  • Increased facial hair.
  • Increased body hair.
  • Thinning and scalp hair loss.
  • Acne
